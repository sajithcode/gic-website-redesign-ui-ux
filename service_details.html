Issuance of National Identity Card for the First Time

Every citizen of Sri Lanka who has reached 15 years of age is required by law to register and obtain a National Identity Card (NIC). The application process is typically facilitated through schools or the Grama Niladhari.

Eligibility

  • Must be a citizen of Sri Lanka.
  • Must have completed 15 years of age.
Note for School Students (O/L Examination) Students preparing for the G.C.E. Ordinary Level examination must apply for their NIC through their school Principal. The Department of Examinations requires a valid NIC to sit for the exam.

Application Process

  1. Obtain the Application Form

    Obtain Form DRP/V/01/01/01 (for age 15+) from your Grama Niladhari, Estate Superintendent (if living in an estate), or School Principal.

  2. Complete Form and Gather Documents

    Fill the form accurately and attach the required supporting documents (see below). You will need photographs meeting DRP specifications.

  3. Certify the Application

    The application must be certified by the Grama Niladhari (countersigned by the Divisional Secretary) OR the School Principal OR the Estate Superintendent.

  4. Submit Application

    Hand over the certified application to the certifying officer, who will forward it to the Department for Registration of Persons through the Divisional Secretariat.

Required Documents

  • Completed application form (DRP/V/01/01/01).
  • Original Birth Certificate or an Extract of the Birth Certificate.
  • Six (06) color photographs (Size: 1 3/8" x 7/8") with a light blue background, taken within the last 3 months by a registered studio.
  • Stamps to the value of Rs. 100.00 (affixed to the application).
  • If seeking to include a profession: Original certificate establishing the profession (e.g., Degree certificate, appointment letter).

Fees and Timelines

Application Fee Rs. 100.00 (paid via stamps affixed to the form)
One-Day Service Fee Rs. 1,000.00 (Optional, available at DRP Head Office only)
Normal Processing Time Approximately 2 to 4 weeks via Divisional Secretariat.
